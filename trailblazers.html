Trailblazers

Dan Price

Dan Price is the Founder and CEO of Gravity Payments, a company involved in processing credit card payments and keeping processing fees low for small businesses. Price made waves in 2015 by announcing a company-wide policy of starting his employees at $70,000 annually.

Girls Who Code

Girls Who Code is a nonprofit organization which aims to support and increase the number of women in computer science by equipping young women with the necessary computing skills to pursue 21st century opportunities.

Rachael Denhollander

Rachael Denhollander is an American lawyer and former gymnast. She was the first woman to publicly accuse Larry Nassar, the former Michigan State University and USA Gymnastics doctor, of sexual assault.

Homeboy Industries

Homeboy Industries is the largest gang intervention, rehabilitation and re-entry program in the world, welcoming thousands through our doors each year. Founded in 1988, Homeboy Industries offers a variety of free wraparound services, such as temporary employment or tattoo removal.

Brandon Copeland

Brandon Copeland is an American football outside linebacker for the New England Patriots of the National Football League (NFL). Having interned at an investment bank during college, he recognizes the need for financial literacy and regularly returns to the University of Pennsylvania to teach his "Life 101" seminar.

Tompkins Conservation

Previously named Conservacion Patagonica, the Tompkins Conservation is named in tribute to Douglas Tompkins, co-founder of the North Face. What started with the purchase of a run-down coastal farm Reñihué fjord in southern Chile has become more than 103,000 acres of conserved land for the public interest of Chile.
